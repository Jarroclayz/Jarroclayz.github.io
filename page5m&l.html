SALES AND BUSINESS IMPACT

Mario & Luigi: Bowsers' Inside Story - Sales and Business Impact

Between 2009, the original release date of the DS version of Mario & Luigi: Bowsers' Inside Story, and March 2023,
the closure of the Nintendo 3DS Eshop, making the game no longer purchasable directly from Nintendo, approximately 4,594,000 were
sold. This number is made up of the 4,560,000 copies of the original DS version, and the 34000 of the 3DS remake.

SALES AND BUSINESS IMPACT

Upon the astronomical success Nintendo saw from releasing a Mario spinoff game, Nintendo continued to employ the third party developer, Alphadream to develop an additional two games:

Mario & Luigi: Dream Team - 2,700,000 units sold

Mario & Luigi: Paper Jam Bros. - 1,080,000 units sold

The immense success Nintendo saw, was one of the first examples of a third party developer taking one of Nintendo's esteemed Intellectual Properties, and making a video game that rivalled the success of first party Nintendo Games.
This encouraged Nintendo to employ a vast variety of third party developers such as:

Next Level Games: Responsible for developing Luigi's Mansion 2 and 3, selling 6,480,000 and 14,250,000 units respectively, one of Nintendo's current most popular series.

Koei Temco: Responsible for developing Fire Emblem Warriors for the Nintendo Switch, selling 3,000,000 units, boosting the series to new heights.

Acquire: Responsible for developing the 2024 title: Mario & Luigi Brothership, a flagship title that revived the Mario & Luigi series, selling roughly 2,000,000 units (sales data not finalised).

Alphadream's Mario & Luigi Bowser's Inside Story is a lovingly, handcrafted experience that encapsulates the power of third party developers. Nintendo ultimately saw this success, and decided to expand on their 3rd party developer profile, resulting in major success.
